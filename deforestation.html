🌳 Deforestation Awareness

Understand the problem, its impact, and how we can help

🚨 What is Deforestation?

Deforestation means cutting down forests for farming, buildings, or logging. It harms nature and wildlife. Every year, over 10 million hectares of forest are lost globally.

🪓 Causes of Deforestation

  • Agriculture – Forests cleared for crops and livestock
  • Logging – Trees cut for wood and paper
  • Urban Growth – Cities expanding into forest areas
  • Fires & Mining – Natural disasters and resource extraction

🌍 Impact on Earth

  • Climate Change – More CO₂, less oxygen
  • Wildlife Loss – Animals lose their homes
  • Soil Erosion – Land becomes weak
  • Water Cycle – Less rain, more drought

🎓 Classroom Activities

  • Storytelling: Narrate a forest’s journey from green to gone
  • Role Play: Act as trees, animals, and humans
  • Poster Making: Create slogans like “Save Trees, Save Life”
  • Tree Diary: Track a tree’s life and benefits

✅ What You Can Do

  • Plant trees in your school or colony
  • Use less paper — go digital
  • Spread awareness with posters and presentations
  • Support forest-friendly products like recycled paper

👥 Social Impact

Deforestation leads to loss of livelihoods for forest communities, conflicts over land, and even health problems due to pollution and heat.
